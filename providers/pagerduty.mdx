## Overview

Connect your users to PagerDuty for incident management, on-call scheduling, alert routing, and service health monitoring.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Provider ID** | `pagerduty` |
| **Category** | Design & Developer Tools |
| **PKCE** | Not supported |
| **Token refresh** | Automatic |
| **Redirect URI** | Shown in [Developer Portal](https://portal.alterai.dev) |

## Step 1: Create a PagerDuty OAuth App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the PagerDuty Developer Portal">
    Navigate to the [PagerDuty OAuth2 documentation](https://developer.pagerduty.com/docs/app-integration-development/oauth-2-functionality/) and sign in to your PagerDuty account.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Register your app">
    Go to **Integrations** > **App Registration** and register a new app. Select **OAuth 2.0** as the authorization type.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure the redirect URI">
    Add the Alter callback URL from the [Developer Portal](https://portal.alterai.dev) to the **Redirect URLs** field.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get your credentials">
    Copy the **Client ID** and **Client Secret**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Never share your Client Secret publicly. Store it securely and only enter it in the Alter Vault Developer Portal.
</Warning>

## Step 2: Add to Alter Vault

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Developer Portal">
    Go to [portal.alterai.dev](https://portal.alterai.dev) and navigate to your app.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add PagerDuty provider">
    Go to **OAuth Providers** > **Add Provider** > **PagerDuty**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter credentials">
    - **Client ID**: Paste your PagerDuty Client ID
    - **Client Secret**: Paste your PagerDuty Client Secret
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select scopes">
    Choose the scopes your app needs.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The provider is now active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Available Scopes

| Scope | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `openid` | OpenID Connect authentication |
| `read` | Read-only access to PagerDuty data |
| `write` | Read and write access to PagerDuty data |

## Notes

- See the [PagerDuty OAuth2 documentation](https://developer.pagerduty.com/docs/app-integration-development/oauth-2-functionality/) for more details.
