## Overview

Connect your users to Contentful for headless CMS content management, content delivery, and space administration.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Provider ID** | `contentful` |
| **Category** | Developer Tools |
| **PKCE** | Supported |
| **Token refresh** | Automatic |
| **Redirect URI** | Shown in [Developer Portal](https://portal.alterauth.com) |

## Step 1: Create a Contentful OAuth App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Contentful OAuth Applications">
    Navigate to [Contentful account settings](https://app.contentful.com/account/profile/developers/applications) and go to **OAuth applications**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new application">
    Click **New Application**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure your app">
    Enter the application name and description.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the redirect URI">
    Add the **Redirect URI** from the [Developer Portal](https://portal.alterauth.com) to the **Redirect URI** field.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get your credentials">
    Copy the **Client ID** and **Client Secret**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Never share your Client Secret publicly. Store it securely and only enter it in the Alter Vault Developer Portal.
</Warning>

## Step 2: Add to Alter Vault

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Developer Portal">
    Go to [portal.alterauth.com](https://portal.alterauth.com) and navigate to your app.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Contentful provider">
    Go to **OAuth Providers** > **Add Provider** > **Contentful**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter credentials">
    - **Client ID**: Paste your Contentful Client ID
    - **Client Secret**: Paste your Contentful Client Secret
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select scopes">
    Choose the scopes your app needs.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The provider is now active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Available Scopes

| Scope | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `content_management_manage` | Full access to the Content Management API |
| `content_management_read` | Read-only access to the Content Management API |

## Notes

- The `content_management_manage` scope is included by default and provides full read/write access.
- Use `content_management_read` if your app only needs to read content.
