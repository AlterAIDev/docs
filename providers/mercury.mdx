## Overview

Connect your users to Mercury for business banking data, account balances, transactions, and financial management.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Provider ID** | `mercury` |
| **Category** | Finance & HR |
| **PKCE** | Not supported |
| **Token refresh** | Automatic |
| **Redirect URI** | Shown in [Developer Portal](https://portal.alterauth.com) |

## Step 1: Create a Mercury OAuth App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Review Mercury's OAuth integration guide">
    Open [Mercury's OAuth2 integration guide](https://docs.mercury.com/docs/integrations-with-oauth2) and submit an integration request. Mercury does not offer a self-serve developer console for OAuth.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Wait for Mercury to provision credentials">
    After approval, Mercury provides a **Client ID** and **Client Secret**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Register the redirect URI">
    Provide the Alter callback URL from the [Developer Portal](https://portal.alterauth.com) as the registered **Redirect URI**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter credentials in Alter Vault">
    Use the **Client ID** and **Client Secret** from Mercury in the Developer Portal (see Step 2 below).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Never share your Client Secret publicly. Store it securely and only enter it in the Alter Vault Developer Portal.
</Warning>

## Step 2: Add to Alter Vault

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Developer Portal">
    Go to [portal.alterauth.com](https://portal.alterauth.com) and navigate to your app.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Mercury provider">
    Go to **OAuth Providers** > **Add Provider** > **Mercury**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter credentials">
    - **Client ID**: Paste your Mercury Client ID
    - **Client Secret**: Paste your Mercury Client Secret
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select scopes">
    Choose the scopes your app needs.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The provider is now active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Available Scopes

| Scope | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `read` | Read-only access to account and transaction data |
| `offline_access` | Maintain continuous access with refresh tokens |

## Notes

- Request only the scopes your application needs. For read-only financial dashboards, `read` is sufficient.
- See the [Mercury OAuth documentation](https://docs.mercury.com/reference/oauth) for more details.
