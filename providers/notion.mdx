## Overview

Connect your users to Notion for notes, documents, databases, and knowledge management.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Provider ID** | `notion` |
| **Category** | Productivity |
| **PKCE** | Not supported |
| **Token refresh** | Not needed (tokens don't expire) |
| **Redirect URI** | Shown in [Developer Portal](https://portal.alterai.dev) |

<Note>
  Notion tokens do not expire and cannot be refreshed. Permissions are granted per-page by the user during authorization.
</Note>

## Step 1: Create a Notion Integration

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Notion Integrations">
    Navigate to [notion.so/my-integrations](https://www.notion.so/my-integrations) and sign in.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new integration">
    Click **New integration**. Select the workspace you want to connect.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set integration type to Public">
    Set the integration type to **Public** (required for OAuth).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add the redirect URI">
    Under **OAuth Domain & URIs**, add the **Redirect URI** from the [Developer Portal](https://portal.alterai.dev) to the **Redirect URIs** field.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get your credentials">
    Copy the **OAuth client ID** and **OAuth client secret**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Never share your Client Secret publicly. Store it securely and only enter it in the Alter Vault Developer Portal.
</Warning>

## Step 2: Add to Alter Vault

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Developer Portal">
    Go to [portal.alterai.dev](https://portal.alterai.dev) and navigate to your app.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Notion provider">
    Go to **OAuth Providers** > **Add Provider** > **Notion**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter credentials">
    - **Client ID**: Paste your Notion OAuth client ID
    - **Client Secret**: Paste your Notion OAuth client secret
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The provider is now active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Available Scopes

Notion does not use traditional OAuth scopes. Instead, permissions are granted per-page by the user during the authorization flow. The user selects which pages and databases to share with your integration.

## Notes

- Notion uses **Internal** and **Public** integration types. You must use **Public** for OAuth.
- Notion tokens do not expire and cannot be refreshed.
- Users grant access to specific pages and databases during authorization, not via scopes.
- See the [Notion OAuth documentation](https://developers.notion.com/docs/authorization) for more details.
