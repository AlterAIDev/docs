## Overview

Connect your users to LinkedIn for professional profile data, social posting, and organizational content.

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Provider ID** | `linkedin` |
| **Category** | Social |
| **PKCE** | Not supported |
| **Token refresh** | Automatic |
| **Redirect URI** | Shown in [Developer Portal](https://portal.alterauth.com) |

## Step 1: Create a LinkedIn OAuth App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the LinkedIn Developer Portal">
    Navigate to [linkedin.com/developers/apps](https://www.linkedin.com/developers/apps) and sign in.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a new app">
    Click **Create app**, fill in the app name, associate it with a LinkedIn Page, and upload a logo.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Configure the redirect URI">
    Under the **Auth** tab, add the Alter callback URL from the [Developer Portal](https://portal.alterauth.com) to **Authorized redirect URLs for your app**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Get your credentials">
    Copy the **Client ID** and **Client Secret** from the **Auth** tab.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Never share your Client Secret publicly. Store it securely and only enter it in the Alter Vault Developer Portal.
</Warning>

## Step 2: Add to Alter Vault

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Developer Portal">
    Go to [portal.alterauth.com](https://portal.alterauth.com) and navigate to your app.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add LinkedIn provider">
    Go to **OAuth Providers** > **Add Provider** > **LinkedIn**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter credentials">
    - **Client ID**: Paste your LinkedIn Client ID
    - **Client Secret**: Paste your LinkedIn Client Secret
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select scopes">
    Choose the scopes your app needs.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. The provider is now active.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Available Scopes

| Scope | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `openid` | OpenID Connect authentication |
| `profile` | Access to profile name, photo, and headline |
| `email` | Access to primary email address |
| `w_member_social` | Post, comment, and like on behalf of member |
| `r_liteprofile` | Read basic profile data |
| `r_emailaddress` | Read email address |
| `r_organization_social` | Read organizational posts and statistics |

## Notes

- LinkedIn requires requesting product access for some scopes (e.g., `r_organization_social`). Apply in the **Products** tab of your app.
- See the [LinkedIn OAuth 2.0 documentation](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/linkedin/shared/authentication/authorization-code-flow) for more details.
